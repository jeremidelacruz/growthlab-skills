## 1. Product
- **One-liner:** Invoicing for freelancers who hate invoicing.
- **What it does:** Turns a finished project into a sent invoice in three taps, chases the
  payment automatically, and reconciles it when the money lands. No accounting knowledge needed.
- **Category:** Freelance invoicing / solo-business billing.
- **Business model & pricing:** Flat $12/mo, one plan, no per-invoice fees. (Source: pricing page.)

## 2. Ideal Customer (ICP)
- **Who they are:** Solo freelancers and one-person studios — designers, writers, developers —
  1 to ~20 clients, billing $2k–$15k/mo, no bookkeeper.
- **Trigger:** Just got paid late (again) and spent a Sunday night rebuilding an invoice in a
  spreadsheet.
- **Job to be done:** (1) Get invoices out the door fast, (2) get paid without nagging clients
  themselves, (3) not think about money admin.
- **Not a fit (anti-persona):** Agencies with a finance person, or anyone who wants full
  double-entry accounting — they'll find it too simple, and that's fine.

## 3. Problem
- **Core problem:** Invoicing is the tax you pay for doing the work you actually like. It's
  fiddly, easy to postpone, and postponing it means getting paid later.
- **Cost of the problem:** Hours per month rebuilding templates; weeks of delay in cash because
  invoices go out late and follow-ups feel awkward.
- **Why current alternatives fall short:** Spreadsheets don't chase payment. Full accounting
  suites (QuickBooks, Xero) solve a bigger problem than a freelancer has and feel like homework.

## 4. Competitors
| Competitor | How they position | Where they fall short for the customer |
|---|---|---|
| Spreadsheet template | Free, familiar | No reminders, no tracking, manual every time |
| QuickBooks Self-Employed | "Run your whole business" | Overkill; steep; built for tax filing, not speed |
| PayPal invoices | Already have the account | Ugly, high fees, no follow-up automation |

## 5. Differentiation
- **What we can say that they can't:** Automatic, polite payment chasing is on by default — the
  freelancer never has to send the "just following up" message themselves.
- **Why that matters:** The awkward part of getting paid isn't making the invoice, it's the
  chasing. Removing that is the whole product.

## 6. Customer Language (verbatim)
- **How they describe the problem:** "I do the work, then I dread the invoice." ·
  "I hate having to nag people for money I already earned."
- **How they describe us:** "It just quietly gets me paid." · "Set it and forget it."
- **Words to use:** get paid, chase (the invoice, not the client), quietly, three taps, on time
- **Words to avoid:** accounts receivable, reconciliation (in customer-facing copy), platform,
  solution, robust
- **Glossary:** "chase" = automated payment reminder sequence.

## 7. Voice
- **Tone:** plainspoken, dry, on-your-side — not peppy, not corporate.
- **Style:** short sentences, second person, no jargon in customer-facing copy.
- **Pairs with:** `design.md` for colors, type, and the written-identity rules.
